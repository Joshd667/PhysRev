Privacy Notice

Last updated: 19 November 2025

Introduction

This Privacy Notice explains how the Physics Knowledge Audit Tool ("we", "our", "the App") collects, uses, and protects your personal information. We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Data Controller

The Physics Knowledge Audit Tool is a client-side Progressive Web Application. Your data is stored locally on your device and is not transmitted to any external servers (except when using Microsoft Teams integration, see below).

What Data We Collect

Learning Progress Data

  • Confidence ratings (1-5 scale) for physics topics
  • Timestamps of when ratings were made
  • Progress analytics and learning metrics

Study Materials

  • Custom notes, flashcards, and mindmaps you create
  • Topic tags you assign to your materials
  • Test results and performance history

Application Preferences

  • View mode preferences (specification/paper view)
  • Theme selection (light/dark mode)
  • Default view settings for notes, flashcards, and mindmaps

Account Information (Teams Users Only)

  • Name and email address (from Microsoft Teams account)
  • Profile picture (if provided by Microsoft Teams)
  • Microsoft Teams user ID

What We Don't Collect

  • No Cookies: We do not use cookies or tracking technologies
  • No Analytics Tracking: We do not use Google Analytics, Segment, or any third-party analytics services
  • No Advertising: We do not collect data for advertising purposes
  • No Location Data: We do not track your physical location
  • No Device Information: We do not collect device identifiers or system information

How We Store Your Data

Guest Mode: All data is stored in IndexedDB on your device. Your data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third parties.

Microsoft Teams Mode: If you choose to use Teams integration, your data is stored using Microsoft's services. Please refer to Microsoft's Privacy Statement for information on how Microsoft handles your data.

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data under the following lawful bases:

  • Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR): Processing is necessary for the provision of educational tools to support your learning
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR): When you choose to use Microsoft Teams integration, you consent to data processing by Microsoft

Data Retention

Analytics History: Automatically deleted after 30 days to minimise data storage

Study Materials: Retained indefinitely until you choose to delete them

Preferences: Retained until you clear them via the Settings menu or browser storage

See our Data Retention Policy for full details.

Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of Access (Article 15):

Export your data as JSON or CSV via Settings → Data Management

Right to Rectification (Article 16):

Edit your confidence ratings, notes, and materials at any time

Right to Erasure (Article 17):

Delete all data via Settings → Data Management → Clear All Data, or by logging out

Right to Data Portability (Article 20):

Export your data in machine-readable formats (JSON, CSV)

Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not share your data with any third parties. In Guest Mode, your data remains entirely on your device. When using Microsoft Teams integration, your data is processed by Microsoft in accordance with their Data Processing Agreement.

Security Measures

We implement the following security measures to protect your data:

  • Content Security Policy (CSP) to prevent XSS attacks
  • DOMPurify sanitization for user-generated content
  • HTTPS-only content delivery
  • No server-side data storage (Guest Mode)
  • Local IndexedDB encryption via browser security features

Children's Privacy

This application is designed for A-Level students (typically aged 16-18). Users under 13 years of age should obtain parental consent before using this application, in accordance with Article 8 of UK GDPR. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13 without parental consent.

Changes to This Privacy Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this Privacy Notice periodically.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Notice or wish to exercise your data protection rights, please contact us through your educational institution or the app administrator.

Complaints to the ICO

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe your data protection rights have been breached.
